Traditional phylogenetic methods rely on the principle of maximum parsimony, which seeks the simplest explanation for determining relationships by minimizing the number of evolutionary changes. By contrast, a Bayesian approach uses a different, probabilistic method that may incorporate the age of fossils (tip dating) into the phylogenetic analysis to estimate divergence times ( 12 ). However, it placed Masripithecus as the stem hominoid that is most closely related to crown hominoids. Al-Ashqar et al. did not analyze the phylogenetic relationship between Masripithecus and Nacholapithecus from Kenya (15 Ma), which has been interpreted as either a stem hominoid ( 10 ) or a hominid ( 13 ). Future phylogenetic analyses that include Nacholapithecus and use other datasets are required to conclusively discount a crown hominoid status for Masripithecus .
